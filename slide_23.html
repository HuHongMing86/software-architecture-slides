Communication – Protocol-Aware Heterogeneous Interoperability

The Microservices Approach
Unlike Service-Oriented Architecture (SOA) which relies on a central Enterprise Service Bus (ESB) for integration, microservices communicate directly with each other without a central integration hub.
Protocol-Aware
Services know how to call each other directly using standard protocols (e.g., REST, gRPC, or message queues). The "smart" routing logic lives in the endpoints, not in a central hub.
Heterogeneous
Because each service is an independent process, they can be written in completely different technology stacks (Java, Go, Node.js) as long as they support the agreed-upon communication protocol.
Interoperability
Services collaborate and exchange data seamlessly to complete complex business workflows, forming a cohesive system from independent parts.
