The Sidecar Pattern Solves Operational Reuse Without Domain Coupling

Separating domain concerns from operational concerns

  • The Dilemma: Microservices prefer duplication over coupling for domain concerns, but operational concerns (monitoring, logging, circuit breakers) genuinely benefit from reuse.
  • The Sidecar Pattern: Each service includes a separate sidecar component handling all operational concerns.
  • Ownership: A shared infrastructure team owns and maintains the sidecar, while the service team owns the domain logic.
  • Upgrades: When upgrades are needed (e.g., new monitoring tool version), the infrastructure team updates the sidecar, and all microservices automatically benefit.
This cleanly separates domain concerns (owned by service teams) from operational concerns (owned by infrastructure teams).
