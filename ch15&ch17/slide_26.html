The Saga Pattern Handles Distributed Transactions with Compensating Actions

What is a Saga?
A sequence of local transactions where each transaction updates data within a single service. If a local transaction fails, the saga executes a series of compensating transactions that undo the changes made by the preceding local transactions.
The Challenge
Traditional ACID distributed transactions (like two-phase commit) are not viable in microservices due to high coupling and severe performance bottlenecks across independent databases.
The Saga Solution
A business workflow is divided into a sequence of independent local transactions. Each service completes its part of the work and publishes an event or message to trigger the next step in the saga.
Compensating Actions
When a local transaction fails, the system cannot perform a standard database rollback. Instead, it triggers a series of compensating transactions to logically reverse the preceding successful steps (e.g., refunding a payment if inventory allocation fails).
