Microservices Architecture – Born from Domain-Driven Design

Core Philosophy
If the architect's goal requires high decoupling, prefer duplication over reuse. The primary goal of microservices is to physically model the logical concept of bounded contexts.
Historical Context
Microservices is a highly popular architecture style that gained massive momentum after Martin Fowler and James Lewis published their seminal blog post "Microservices" in March 2014.
Key Inspiration: Bounded Context
The architecture is heavily inspired by Domain-Driven Design (DDD), particularly the concept of the Bounded Context.

Each context is strictly decoupled, containing only what it needs internally (code, data schema) without coupling to anything outside its boundary.
