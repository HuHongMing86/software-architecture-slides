Data Isolation – Each Service Owns Its Own Data

Microservices avoid all types of coupling, including shared schemas and databases used as integration points.

  • No Single Source of Truth: There is no central shared relational database.
  • Data Distribution: Architects must decide whether to designate one domain as the source of truth and coordinate with it, or use database replication/caching to distribute information.
  • Polyglot Persistence: Each service can choose the most appropriate database technology (relational, document, graph, key-value) based on its specific needs.
Benefit: Teams in highly decoupled systems can switch to a more suitable database without impacting other teams – a major advantage over monolithic architectures.
