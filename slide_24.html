Choreography Maximizes Decoupling but Complicates Error Handling

Choreography
A communication style where services interact by publishing and subscribing to events without a central orchestrator. Each service acts independently based on the events it receives.
Pros (Strengths)
  • Maximum Decoupling: Services are completely unaware of each other, relying only on shared event schemas.
  • High Responsiveness: Asynchronous event-driven communication reduces latency.
  • Scalability: No central orchestrator means no single bottleneck or point of failure.
Cons (Weaknesses)
  • Complex Error Handling: Without a central controller, managing failures, retries, and timeouts is highly distributed and difficult.
  • Workflow Tracking: It is challenging to track the state of a business transaction across multiple independent services.
  • Compensating Actions: Reverting changes when a downstream service fails requires complex, custom logic in each participating service.
