Orchestration Simplifies Complex Workflows at the Cost of Coupling

Orchestration
A communication style where a central mediator (orchestrator) coordinates the workflow, explicitly calling individual services to perform their tasks and managing the overall state of the transaction.
Pros (Strengths)
  • Centralized Error Handling: Easy to manage failures, retries, and timeouts from a single location.
  • Workflow Tracking: The orchestrator knows the exact state of the business transaction at any given time.
  • State Management: Greatly simplifies complex workflows that require compensating actions or rollbacks.
Cons (Weaknesses)
  • High Coupling: The orchestrator becomes highly coupled to the APIs and contracts of all participating services.
  • Bottleneck Risk: The central orchestrator can become a performance bottleneck under heavy load.
  • Single Point of Failure: If the orchestrator goes down, the entire workflow halts, requiring high availability setups.
